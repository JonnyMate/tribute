Ritchie Blackmore

The man who wrote Smoke on the Water

Blackmore playing his signature Fender Stratocaster

Short Biography

1945

Born "Richard Hugh Blackmore" on April 14th 1945 in Weston-super-mare, Somerset.

1956

Given his first guitar by his father on the condition that he learnt to play it properly and recieved classical guitar lessons.

1960

Began session work for Joe Meek's music productions. Initially a member of The Outlaws. Then went on to join Screaming Lord Sutch and The Savages.

1968

Co-founded Deep Purple with Jon Lord. Mk I lineup consisted of Ritchie Blackmore (Guitar), Jon Lord(Keyboard), Ian Paice (Drums), Rod Evans (Vocals) and Nick Simper (Bass).

1970

Deep Purple replaced Evans and Simper with Ian Gillan and Roger Glover (of Episode VI) respectively. This Mk II lineup was, arguably, the most successful due to the band finally finding a direction in their music.

1972

Following the burning down of a casino in Montruex, the band wrote the song Smoke on the Water. The iconic riff is a staple in rock music and is often one of the first things taught to learning guitarists.

1973

Gillan and Roger replaced with David Coverdale and Glenn Hughes respectively.

1975

Blackmore left Deep Purple to front his new group, Rainbow. He was replaced in Deep Purple by Tommy Bolin

1984

Rainbow had become vastly successful, but the Mk II lineup of Deep Purple was reformed and released the album "Perfect Strangers". The reunion lasted until 1989 when Joe Lynn Turner (vocalist of Rainbow) came and replaced Gillan.

1992

The Mk II lineup reunited for a second time and released the album "The Battle Rages On"

1993

Blackmore left Deep Purple for good and reformed Rainbow.

1997

Blackmore began to move towards more traditional folk music with his wife. They created Blackmore's Night which debuted with "Shadow of the Moon".

Present

While Blackmore mainly plays folk music, there are still hints of electric guitar in his work and has even performed with Rainbow and played his old songs.

"When you're around someone good, your own standards are raised." - Ritchie Blackmore
